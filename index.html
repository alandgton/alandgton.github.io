Welcome to Comp Sci 162

What is Comp Sci 162?

CS 162 is UC Berkeley's upper-division computer science course for Operating Systems & Systems Programming.

Over the span of 15 weeks, students work in teams of 4 to design and implement features for PintOS. Check out the course website at cs162.eecs.berkeley.edu.

What do you do?

I teach two 1-hour discussion sections every week. I create my own slides and post them here (some material sourced from lecture slides, old discussion slides from previous TAs, the internet, and the textbook).

In the future, I plan on posting notes, diagrams, and videos here as well.

Notes

CS162-relevant content extracted from Operating Systems: Principles & Practice by Anderson & Dahlin, Advanced Programming in the UNIX Environment by Stevens & Rago, Linux Kernel Development by Robert Love, the Internet, and my head.
